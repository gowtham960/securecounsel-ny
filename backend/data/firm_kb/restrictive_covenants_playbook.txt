Title: Firm Restrictive Covenants Playbook

When reviewing restrictive covenants, attorneys should assess duration, geographic scope, legitimate business interest, employee role, consideration, and whether the restriction is narrowly tailored.

New York courts generally scrutinize employee non-compete agreements closely. A covenant that is overbroad in time, geography, or restricted activity may be partially enforced, narrowed, or rejected depending on facts and current law.

For employment agreements, attorneys should separately analyze non-competition, non-solicitation, confidentiality, garden leave, trade secret protection, and client relationship restrictions.

A two-year statewide restriction for a sales employee may raise enforceability concerns if the employer cannot show a legitimate business interest and narrow tailoring.
